Short Biography

    Dr. Fan Li is currently a professor in the School of Computer Science and Technology in Beijing Institute of Technology. She received her BEng in Information Technology and her MEng degrees in Communications and Information System from Huazhong University of Science and Technology in 1998 and 2001, respectively, the MEng degree in Electrical Engineering from the University of Delaware, USA in 2004. In the year 2008, she received the PhD degree from the University of North Carolina at Charlotte, USA.

    Dr. Li has published more than 200 refereed papers in international leading journals and key conferences in the areas of wireless communications and networking, mobile computing, and security & privacyis, such as ACM MOBICOM, IEEE INFOCOM, ACM UbiComp, and IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing, IEEE Transactions on Dependable and Secure Computing, IEEE Transactions on Services Computing. She is the inventor of more than 40 granted and 20 pending patents.

    Her current research interests include smart sensing, cyber security and privacy, Internet of Things (IoT), smart healthcare and mobile computing.

Research Focus

  • Smart Sensing: integrates advanced technologies for human-computer interaction and environmental perception. It includes hand movement tracking, gesture recognition, speech/Braille detection, and driver behavior analysis to enhance automation and user experience. By leveraging innovative sensing methods, it provides precise and real-time monitoring capabilities, supporting applications in interactive systems, safety monitoring, and accessibility.
  • Cyber security and Privacy: integrates multi-modal identity verification (e.g., heartbeat, bone conduction, gesture signals) with privacy-preserving technologies to create robust, secure authentication frameworks. These solutions address challenges in data protection and access control, offering enhanced system security for modern digital ecosystems, including personal devices and cloud environments.
  • Internet of Things (IoT): focuses on creating intelligent, connected systems for diverse applications. It includes indoor positioning, resource optimization, and AI-driven IoT solutions such as smart transportation. These innovations aim to improve operational efficiency and user experience by seamlessly integrating intelligent devices and networks.
  • Smart Healthcare: employs sensing and AI technologies for real-time health monitoring (e.g., fitness tracking, blood pressure, ECG), hygiene recognition, and fatigue detection. These solutions provide personalized and preventive healthcare, improving diagnostic accuracy and supporting health management across diverse scenarios.
